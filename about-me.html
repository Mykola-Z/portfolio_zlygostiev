Hi, my name is Oleksandr Hryhorenko. I have completed my Ph.D. (accelerator physics) in December of 2019 at the University of Paris Sud under the supervision of Dr. Marin Chabot. The aim of my Ph.D. was to study an alternative path to reduce the cost of construction of future accelerators like FCC and ILC. In particular, my area of research was related to material science and mechanical polishing of superconducting accelerating cavities (one of the main components of the accelerator). I investigated the metallographic and centrifugal barrel polishing of the Niobium, which is used as the main material to build the superconducting radio-frequency (SRF) accelerating cavities. In this thesis, the work consisted mainly in assessing the chemical cleanliness, crystallographic quality of the polished surface, nanoroughness, abrasion rate, while simplifying the usual centrifugal barrel polishing and metallographic techniques so that process is compatible with industrialization and could be used for large scale production. Hence, optical inspection, abrasion rate measurement, pollution characterization, and crystallographic have been done. The surfaces were characterized with the several techniques or devices: in particular laser confocal microscope (mainly roughness measurement), SIMS (Secondary Ion Mass Spectrometer), GIXRD (Grazing Incidence X-Ray Diffractometer), SEM (Scanning Electron Microscope) and EBSD (Electron-BackScattered Diffraction). In order to simplify data characterization (de-pollution studies, roughness evolution and recognition of SIMS peaks), scripts have been written in Python language (with pandas, numpy, plotly and matplotlib and etc. packages). Hence, these were the first steps to data science direction.
